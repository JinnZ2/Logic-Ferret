Philosophy of the Score
	•	Not all distortion is equal.
	•	Some tactics (like false urgency) are annoying.
	•	Others (like agency removal or propaganda bias) are existential threats to autonomy.

So we assign weights based on how much the distortion disrupts clarity and choice.

⸻

 Default Weights (tweakable in truth_integrity_score.py):

Sensor                        Weight  Why
Propaganda Tone               1.2     Influences emotional tone and worldview
Reward Manipulation           1.0     Hooks attention, but often superficial
False Urgency                 1.1     Triggers panic, blocks rational response
Gatekeeping                   1.3     Denies access to knowledge, preserves power
Narrative Fragility           1.4     Collapses coherence, hides truth behind fog
Propaganda Bias               1.5     Persuades through distortion of reality
Agency Restriction            1.6     Removes consent, simulates choice (highest threat)
Fallacy Overlay               1.3     Classic logical fallacies undermine reasoning
Gaslight Frequency            1.5     Reality distortion erodes trust in perception
Responsibility Deflection     1.2     Blame shifting obscures accountability
True Accountability*          1.0     Positive signal — inverted in composite scoring
Meritocracy*                  1.1     Positive signal — inverted in composite scoring

⸻

Scoring Direction:
	•	Most sensors: higher score = more distortion detected (bad)
	•	Positive sensors (*): higher score = more accountability/merit (good)
	  These are automatically inverted by the C3 calculator so that
	  high accountability *reduces* the composite distortion score.
